OASIS All-Payer Reporting 2026: What Home Health Agencies Need to Do Now

Published April 2026  ·  HomeHealthBillingClarity.com

Starting January 1, 2026, home health agencies must complete OASIS assessments for all patients — regardless of payer. This all-payer reporting expansion is one of the most operationally significant changes in the CY 2026 CMS Home Health Final Rule. Here's what it means and what your agency needs to do.

📋 Illustration: OASIS assessment form with all-payer icons — Medicare, Medicaid, private insurance — sage green editorial style

What Is OASIS and Why Does It Matter?

OASIS — Outcome and Assessment Information Set — is the standardized patient assessment tool used in home health care. It captures information about a patient's functional status, clinical condition, and care needs at key points in their episode of care: admission, recertification, and discharge.

OASIS data has two main functions. First, it drives Medicare payment — the OASIS assessment determines a patient's Home Health Resource Group (HHRG) classification, which determines reimbursement. Second, it feeds into CMS quality reporting systems, which affect an agency's star ratings, public reporting, and value-based purchasing scores.

Before 2026, OASIS was required only for Medicare and Medicaid patients. Private pay patients, managed care patients billed outside of Medicare, and other non-Medicare/Medicaid payers were excluded from the OASIS requirement.

What Changed in 2026

The 2026 CMS Home Health Final Rule expanded OASIS reporting to all payers. Effective January 1, 2026, every patient admitted to a Medicare-certified home health agency requires a complete OASIS assessment at:

  • Admission (Start of Care)
  • Recertification (if care continues beyond the initial episode)
  • Transfer to an inpatient facility
  • Resumption of care after an inpatient stay
  • Discharge

This requirement applies regardless of whether the patient is billed under Medicare, Medicaid, private insurance, managed care, or self-pay.

⚠️ If You Have Private Pay or Managed Care Patients

If your agency has historically served private pay or managed care patients without completing OASIS assessments, you are now out of compliance with the 2026 all-payer mandate. This needs to be addressed immediately — not at your next compliance review.

Why CMS Expanded OASIS to All Payers

CMS's stated rationale for the all-payer expansion is standardization and data completeness. By requiring OASIS for all patients regardless of payer, CMS can build a more complete picture of home health outcomes across the entire patient population — not just the Medicare subset.

From a practical standpoint, this also creates a more consistent clinical workflow. Instead of staff needing to track which patients require OASIS and which don't, the answer becomes simple: everyone does.

The Operational Impact on Small Agencies

For large agencies that already complete OASIS for most patients as a standard clinical practice, the all-payer mandate is a minor adjustment. For smaller agencies that explicitly carved out non-Medicare patients from OASIS workflows, the impact is more significant.

Areas to review immediately:

  • Intake processes: Does your admission workflow trigger OASIS completion for all patients, or only Medicare/Medicaid?
  • Software configuration: Is your billing or clinical software set up to generate OASIS assessments for non-Medicare payers?
  • Staff training: Do your clinicians know that OASIS is now required for all patients, including private pay?
  • Discharge documentation: Are you completing discharge OASIS assessments for all patient types?
  • Data submission: Are you set up to submit OASIS data to the HAVEN or iQIES system for all patients?

OASIS Data Submission: What You Need to Know

OASIS data must be submitted electronically to CMS through the iQIES (Internet Quality Improvement and Evaluation System) portal. If your agency is only set up to submit OASIS for Medicare patients, you may need to update your submission processes to include all-payer records. Contact your OASIS software vendor if you're unsure whether your system supports all-payer submission.

OASIS Documentation Quality: What Surveyors Look For

Beyond the expansion of which patients require OASIS, the 2026 rules also maintain strict standards for the quality and accuracy of OASIS documentation. Common OASIS deficiencies that surveyors flag include:

  • Inconsistencies between the OASIS assessment and the plan of care
  • Inaccurate functional status ratings that don't match clinical notes
  • Missing or late start-of-care assessments
  • Discharge assessments not completed within required timeframes
  • OASIS items completed by uncredentialed staff

All-Payer OASIS Compliance Checklist

  • Confirm OASIS is triggered for all new admissions regardless of payer
  • Update intake forms and software to remove Medicare-only OASIS filters
  • Train all clinical staff on the all-payer OASIS requirement
  • Review recertification processes for non-Medicare patients
  • Confirm discharge OASIS is completed for all patient types
  • Verify your iQIES submission setup handles all-payer records
  • Audit a sample of recent non-Medicare patient records for OASIS completion
